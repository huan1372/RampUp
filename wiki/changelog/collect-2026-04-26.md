## Key Findings

### 1. FlashInfer NVLink Crash in DP+EP Configurations (PR #40893)

This is the most operationally significant fix of the day. The FlashInfer NVLink MNNVL workspace bug would cause an **assertion failure at engine startup** for any multi-node Blackwell deployment combining DP and EP where `dp_size != ep_size`. The DP=4, TP=2, EP configuration tested on Kimi-K2.5-NVFP4 is typical for large-scale production MoE serving on B200 clusters.

The bug is silent until deployment — it would only surface on configurations mixing DP>1 with EP>1 at actual scale. Prior runs' EPLB and NIXL EP fixes (PR #40810, #40412) made this bug more reachable by increasing deployment diversity.

**Impact category:** Production blocker for affected configurations, not a performance regression.

### 2. HMA KV Offload Series Progresses (PR #39403)

Part 11 of the HMA KV offload series extends `build_connector_meta` to handle multi-group KV store operations. This is prerequisite infrastructure for enabling KV cache offloading on HMA-class architectures — including DeepSeek V4's CSA/HCA multi-tier KV layout. The series represents ongoing groundwork for making vLLM's KV offloading work with next-generation attention architectures.

The prior multi-group PR (#38453, April 22) handled the transfer path; #39403 handles the store path. More parts likely remain.

### 3. DiP-SD: Edge Speculative Decoding Is a Different Design Space

DiP-SD (arXiv 2604.20919) is the first SD paper in this KB that explicitly targets **multi-user edge inference** rather than datacenter single-server inference. Its two innovations — distributed drafting (N devices in parallel) and draft-verify pipeline parallelism — are only possible in the edge setting where draft and verify are on different nodes.

This paper matters for the KB not because it's integrable into vLLM immediately, but because it shows SD is being extended to settings where vLLM's co-location assumption breaks. As on-device LLMs become more capable, edge/hybrid serving architectures may converge with datacenter patterns.

**Limitation in coverage:** arXiv HTML returned 403 on all fetch attempts; specific speedup numbers not captured. Full quantitative results remain an open question.

### 4. MoE Unpad Fix Reveals GPT-OSS B200 Was Broken (PR #40865)

The MoE routed output unpadding fix shows that **GPT-OSS 20B on B200 was non-functional** due to a non-contiguous tensor crash during GPQA evaluation. This was introduced when the unpadding logic was generalized for Nemotron-Nano-v3 without handling the case of architectures without shared experts. The conditional fix covers both cases correctly.
