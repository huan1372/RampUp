## Key Findings

### 1. MXFP4 W4A4 CUTLASS MoE Kernel for SM100 (PR #37463, merged April 2026)

The most significant vLLM change in the April 17-19 window. vLLM now has a CUTLASS-based W4A4 MXFP4 kernel for MoE expert GEMMs on Blackwell B200 (SM100). Key points:

- **W4A4 means maximum quantization**: both weights AND activations are in 4-bit MXFP4 format during the expert GEMM. This is more aggressive than the existing NVFP4 W4A16 (which only quantizes weights)
- **CUTLASS vs FlashInfer**: the previous MXFP4 MoE path used FlashInfer. CUTLASS enables deeper SM100-specific tuning (WGMMA instructions, TMA, warpgroup scheduling). Hugging Face benchmarks suggested FlashInfer had a meaningful TFLOPS gap vs optimized CUTLASS on FP4 MoE
- **SM100-only**: Blackwell B200/GB200 — not SM120 (DGX Spark, RTX PRO 6000)
- **Pre-release**: not yet in a numbered vLLM release; build from source required
- **New wiki page**: `wiki/techniques/fp4-quantization.md` created to cover the full FP4 story (NVFP4, MXFP4 W4A4, hardware landscape)

### 2. TurboQuant WHT Overhead Reduction (PR #40194, merged April 2026)

Refinement to the April 15 TurboQuant merge. Removes a redundant random sign flip from the Walsh-Hadamard Transform pipeline — a step that was theoretically unnecessary (WHT provides channel decorrelation without random signs). This reduces per-token KV quantization compute without changing compression presets or quality. Modest throughput improvement expected at all context lengths for TurboQuant users.

### 3. Calibrated Speculative Decoding (arXiv 2604.13634, April 15, 2026)

Research from SJTU and Alibaba Cloud. CSD tackles false rejections in standard speculative decoding via two training-free modules. OCM tracks historical rejections to propose lexically alternative but semantically equivalent rescue tokens; SCG validates rescues via probability ratios. 2.33× peak throughput speedup. Not yet in vLLM — integration requires changes to the rejection sampler. Added to `speculative-decoding.md`.

### 4. Multimodal Scheduler Overhead (PR #40143, merged April 2026)

Reduces `get_num_embed` call overhead in the multimodal scheduling path. Practical improvement for operators running vision-language workloads (LLaVA, Gemma4 vision, InternVL) at high request rates. Noted in KV cache and speculative-decoding pages as an operational improvement; not warranting a standalone wiki page.
