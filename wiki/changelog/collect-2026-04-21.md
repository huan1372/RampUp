### 2. YOCO++ (arXiv 2604.13556, April 15, 2026) — Missed in April 16 Collect

The April 16 collect reported "no new papers confirmed from April 15–16." YOCO++ was submitted April 15 and was overlooked. It is the most architecturally novel KV compression paper in the collection:

- Builds on YOCO (cross-layer KV sharing): top-half Transformer layers share a single global KV from the middle layer
- YOCO++ adds learned weighted residual connections between bottom-half layer KVs and the input layer KV
- Caches combined KVs to avoid inference-time I/O overhead
- Achieves SOTA among cross-layer methods at 50% KV reduction
- Requires model retraining — not applicable to existing model weights
- Not yet supported in vLLM; would require architectural changes to the KV cache manager

### 3. FP16 KV Cache Numerical Divergence (arXiv 2604.15409, April ~18–20, 2026)

The most impactful correctness finding ingested to date:

- FP16 KV-cached inference is **not numerically equivalent** to cache-free recomputation
- Root cause: FP16 non-associativity creates different accumulation orderings for cached vs. non-cached paths
- Empirical: 100% token divergence rate across all models and sampling strategies on GSM8K
- But cache-ON yields **higher accuracy in 8 of 9 conditions** — divergence is systematic and favorable
- FP32 reduces divergence by 8 orders of magnitude; FP32 flip rate = 0%
- Architecture patterns: GQA models (Mistral, LLaMA-2) diverge at layer 0; Gemma-2 shows uniform drift across layers

**Key implications for this knowledge base:**
1. Any correctness test comparing cache-ON vs. cache-OFF in FP16 will show 100% divergence even for bug-free implementations
2. KV quantization quality benchmarks (FP8, TurboQuant) measure divergence over the baseline FP16 divergence, not over theoretical exact computation
3. The "FP8 KV is near-zero quality impact" claim remains empirically valid but means: marginal additional divergence over the FP16 baseline
